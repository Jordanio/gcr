Accessibility

Recognizing disability as an element of diversity and funding disability programs and services exemplifies the University’s commitment to equality of opportunity for students, faculty, and staff.  The University is home to 18 disability research and resource centers.

Say Yes to Education

A landmark collaboration–one that brings Syracuse University together with dozens of governmental, philanthropic and corporate organizations to ensure that all Syracuse public school students can afford, and succeed in, college.

SubCAT

A 10,000-square-foot professional sound recording complex in downtown Syracuse, is a popular destination for local recording artists, bands, and musical producers.  During the summer months, SubCat plays host to The Inclusive Recording Studio, an intensive six-week summer course in which music education graduate students teach people with disabilities—most of them area high school students—to operate the studio’s recording equipment.

| Read More

Syracuse University's School of Education

A national leader in inclusive urban education.  Its many programs, research and service projects related to inclusive education are leading the way toward greater inclusion for individuals with disabilities and other students who have historically been marginalized in the educational system.

|

All Star Cast

A program of the College of Visual and Performing Arts that provides a safe, non-judgmental space for community actors and drama students to come together to explore their creativity; to encourage communication, group awareness, sensitivity, sharing, confidence, and personal affirmation; and to re-awaken a sense of “play” and joy in acting, when out of the competitive arena.
